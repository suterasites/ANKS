Our process

From first consultation to final handover.

  1. Consultation

    On-site consultation

    Every successful project begins with understanding the site, project goals and construction requirements. We meet on-site to discuss opportunities, constraints, budgets, timelines and objectives before providing recommendations tailored to the project.

  2. Planning

    Design & planning

    Where required, we work alongside clients, architects, designers and consultants to coordinate plans, specifications, approvals and construction methodology. This stage ensures all landscape elements are properly considered before construction begins.

  3. Construction

    Construction commencement

    With planning complete, our team begins site preparation, excavation, infrastructure and landscape construction works. We maintain clear communication throughout while coordinating trades, materials and programme delivery.

  4. Finishing

    Finishing touches

    Once major construction works are complete, planting, turf installation, irrigation systems and landscape detailing are completed. This stage transforms the constructed framework into a finished outdoor environment ready for occupation.

  5. Handover

    Handover & establishment

    Final site presentation, quality assurance inspections and project handover are completed. Clients receive maintenance guidance, warranty information and ongoing support to ensure the landscape establishes successfully and performs as intended.

Questions?
We have answers.

Do you work alongside our architect or designer?

Yes. We regularly collaborate with architects, building designers and consultants throughout the planning and construction process.

Early involvement allows us to provide practical construction input, identify opportunities and ensure the landscape integrates seamlessly with the architecture from concept through to completion.

Do you handle construction and earthworks, or only planting?

All of it, in-house. Landscape, construction (pools, retaining, terraces, pergolas) and earthworks (site preparation, drainage, levels) sit under one team.

That is deliberate. It means the disciplines align from day one rather than being subcontracted apart and stitched together at the end.

How long does a project take?

It depends on scope, site access and how much structural work sits beneath the surface. Levels, drainage, retaining and soil all need to be right before planting goes in, so a considered project is rarely a quick job. We give you a realistic programme at proposal stage, not an optimistic one.

What does a project cost?

Every site is different, so we do not publish a fixed price. After an initial site visit and brief we prepare a detailed proposal, and we are upfront about where the budget goes. If cost certainty matters to you, we build the proposal around it.
